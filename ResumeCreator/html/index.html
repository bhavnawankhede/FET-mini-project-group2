Templates

These are common resume formats you can pick from:

Reverse Chronological

The chronological resume - also known as the “reverse chronological resume” - is the most popular resume format out there. Particularly advisable for those with rich work history, the chronological resume prioritizes and lists your work experience and achievements from most to least recent. This article is here to teach you all there is to know about creating a chronological resume.

Functional

Combination (Hybrid)

So, what is a combination resume exactly and what does it combine? Essentially, a combination resume, aka a hybrid resume, combines 2 traditional resume formats. It’s a mix of the reverse-chronological resume and the functional resume format. As in, it places equal emphasis both on your skills and work experience.

Essential Tips

Essential tips to Keep in mind for a Professional Resume Format

Single Page

We recommend keeping your resume length short. Avoid writing long, 2-3 pages resume when not necessary. No one is free and interested in reading the long journey of your professional life

Break resume into sections

Always divide your resume into proper sections like contact information, skills, summary, work experience

Create a header

Create a header for the contact information section

Keep it concise

Using lengthy statements and paragraphs can make it look odd and annoying. So, keep it straight to the point statements

Avoid making grammatical and spelling errors

The resume errors like grammatical and spelling irritate anybody reading it. Grammatical mistakes can change the whole meaning of a sentence. It can give an employer the wrong ideas about you. So, be careful and avoid making grammatical errors

Don't add a photo

Don't add your photo unless specified or asked for it
